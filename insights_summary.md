# Premier League Performance Analytics - Key Findings (2019/20 - 2023/24)

## Finding 1: COVID and Home Advantage

To analyse the effect of COVID on performance, we observed the home win rate across seasons. We can see that it went from 45.3%, in 2019/20 where teams played with fans until March 2020 when the season resumed behind closed doors, to 37.9% in 2020/21 season, where there were no fans, before steadily increasing post COVID, with a peak of 48.4% in 2022/23. In 2020/21 season, we can also see that the average home goals dropped to 1.353, converged with the average away goals at 1.342, as if there was no difference whether a team was home or away. These show that the home advantage is indeed an influence on the home team, being able to boost the home team to perform better, score more goals and have a higher win rate. 

## Finding 2: Shot conversion and league position

We can observe in two seasons, 2019/20 and 2020/21 season, Tottenham Hotspur ranked second and first respectively in shot conversion, yet they finished sixth and fourth respectively in both seasons. We also see that in both 2021/22 and 2023/24 seasons, West Ham United were ranked second in shot conversion but finished in seventh and ninth place respectively. This goes to say that while shot conversion plays a big role in scoring goals and winning games, having a high conversion rate alone doesn't seem to be enough to finish high in the table. This can be due to potentially the teams having a high conceding rate, conceding more than they score, or that these teams take fewer shots overall, inflating their conversion rate.

## Finding 3: Highest scoring head to heads

Having filtered for at least 4 head to heads, we can see that Manchester City vs Watford is the highest scoring fixture in the dataset, averaging 5.5 goals per game over the 4 games played. Majority of the high rankings have faced similar number of times, but the notable standouts are Leicester City vs Tottenham Hotspur having met 8 times and averaged 4.5 goals per game, or even Newcastle United vs West Ham United having met 10 times across 5 seasons while averaging 4.3 goals per game. These are certainly interesting observations and with a bigger dataset, can be of interest to bettors or avid football fans itching to witness many goals in a game. 

## Limitation of the analysis

Our analysis was based on 5 seasons across 2019/20 to 2023/24. Relatively speaking this is a small dataset to be able to construct evidence-backed analysis and decisions. In addition, for the COVID analysis, a strong assumption was made that is the parallel trend assumption. In order to do difference-in-differencing, we had to assume that the trend of the average home win rate before COVID was parallel to post COVID. A bigger dataset is needed to ensure this assumption holds. We also note that COVID occured in the midst of the 2019/20 season, matches from March 2020 onwards were played behind closed doors after the restart, meaning our pre-COVID baseline are slightly contaminated.
