# Premier League Performance Analytics - Key Findings (2019/20 - 2023/24)

## Finding 1: COVID and Home Advantage

To analyse the effect of COVID on performance, we observed the home win rate across seasons. It fell from 45.3% in 2019/20, where teams played with fans until March 2020 when the season resumed behind closed doors, to 37.9% in 2020/21, where there were no fans at all. After fans returned, win rates steadily recovered and peaked at 48.4% in 2022/23. In 2020/21, average home goals dropped to 1.353 and converged with average away goals at 1.342, as if there was no difference whether a team was home or away. Taken together, these figures suggest home advantage is a real influence on performance rather than just background noise.

**Caveat:** The 2019/20 baseline is slightly contaminated. Matches from March 2020 onwards were played behind closed doors after the restart, which compresses the apparent pre-COVID advantage and likely makes the true effect larger than the raw numbers show.

## Finding 2: Shot Conversion and League Position

In 2019/20 and 2020/21, Tottenham Hotspur ranked second and first respectively in shot-on-target conversion, yet finished sixth and fourth in the table. In 2021/22 and 2023/24, West Ham United ranked second in conversion but finished seventh and ninth. This suggests that while shot conversion plays a big role in scoring goals, a high conversion rate alone is not enough to finish high in the table. It could be that these teams have a high conceding rate, or that they take fewer shots overall, inflating their conversion figure without the volume to match.

## Finding 3: Highest-Scoring Head to Heads

Having filtered for at least four meetings to avoid small-sample noise, Manchester City vs Watford is the highest-scoring fixture in the dataset, averaging 5.5 goals per game across four games. Most highly-ranked pairings have met a similar number of times, but the standouts are Leicester City vs Tottenham Hotspur (8 meetings, 4.5 goals per game) and Newcastle United vs West Ham United (10 meetings across all five seasons, 4.3 goals per game). With a larger dataset, these patterns would be of genuine interest to bettors and broadcasters looking for high-scoring fixtures.

## Limitations

The analysis covers 1,900 matches across five seasons, which is a relatively small dataset for drawing firm conclusions. The COVID analysis relies on a parallel-trends assumption that the pre-COVID home win rate trend would have continued at the same rate absent the intervention. A longer historical series would be needed to verify this holds.
